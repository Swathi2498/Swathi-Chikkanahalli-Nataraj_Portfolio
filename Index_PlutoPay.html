PlutoPay

Project Summary

"A responsive web app in which users are allowed to shop, transfer money, and more without a debit or credit card or the need to visit a physical bank or store. This app helps the users to track their expenses and also help them to create a categorize of their spending into different wallets (transportation, food, etc.) so users' know exactly where their money goes, they can set a limit to take control of their expenditures and also allows users to invest and manage their money in various financial markets, including stocks, bonds, mutual funds and cryptocurrencies".

Hypothesis Statement

PlutoPay users need a secure and seamless way to transfer money without relying on traditional card methods, which can be slow and inconvenient. They also lack an integrated budget planner with notifications for over or under-budget spending, as well as tools to effectively track and manage their expenditures. Additionally, users interested in the stock market face limited access to relevant information within the app, making it difficult to make informed investment decisions.

Design Process Thinking

By empathizing with users, defining the right problem, generating diverse ideas, prototyping, testing, and iterating, design thinking enables designers to create meaningful and impactful solutions.

User Research

By conducting user research, designers can uncover valuable insights that inform the creation of user-centered and impactful solutions. By conducting user interviews and a survey with five participants, I gained valuable insights from diverse perspectives, unearthing novel solutions to incorporate into my design.

Key insights

  • Understanding user behaviors and preferences regarding international and local money transfers, budgeting practices, and investment preferences. This includes identifying common financial goals, spending patterns,and the specific needs users have when it comes to managing their finances across borders.
  • Evaluating the user experience and interface of the app to ensure it is intuitive, user-friendly, and efficient for conducting international and local money transfers. This includes streamlining the transaction process,providing clear instructions, and minimizing friction points to enhance user satisfaction.
  • Assessing how users engage with budgeting and expense tracking features. This includes understanding their desired level ofgranularity in budgeting, the ease of entering and categorizing expenses, and identifying ways to provide meaningful insights and recommendations for effective financial management.
  • Exploring users' expectations and preferences for investment features within the app. This involves identifying the desired investment options, ease of portfolio management, access to relevant investment information, and educational resourcesto empower users to make informed investment decisions.
  • Investigating users' trust and security concerns when using the app for financial transactions and investment activities.This includes addressing security measures,data protection, and establishing transparent communication to build user trust in the app's capabilities.

Usability Testing

After creating the wireframes, I progressed to prototype my design using Figma. To gather valuable feedback and insights, I recruited potential app users to participate in user testing sessions and evaluate the prototype's usability and effectiveness. To assess the user-friendliness and intuitive nature of my design, I conducted usability tests with six participants. Their objective was to complete a series of tasks on PlutoPay, including onboarding, international money transfers, creating budget categories, and accessing investment information. Through their valuable feedback, I identified critical usability issues and friction points, allowing me to prioritize improvements and optimize the overall user experience. To effectively analyze the extensive amount of information gathered, I utilized an additional affinity map (and acquired additional sticky notes as needed). With the aid of a Rainbow spreadsheet, I systematically organized and categorized the data,enabling a comprehensive understanding of the insights obtained.

Usability Test Report

The usability test report focused on evaluating the user experience of PlutoPay's key features, including onboarding, international money transfers, creating budget categories, and accessing investment information. Through the testing process, various tasks were assigned to participants to gauge the effectiveness and efficiency of these functionalities. The report presents the findings and results obtained from the tests, providing insights into the strengths and weaknesses of the app's usability. In addition to the usability tests, a preference test was conducted to gather insights into user preferences and perceptions related to PlutoPay's features. This test aimed to understand user preferences regarding design elements, layout, color schemes, and overall user interface preferences. Two preference tests were conducted on the onboarding and homepage screens which helps to determine the insight into understanding mental models across the target audience, which can, in turn, helps in making a better design decisions. The testing was completed via UsabilityHub and received 13 responses for each test. After reviewing the choices made and the reasons provided, adjustments were made to both preferred designs.

UI Design and Implementation

PlutoPay, an online payment app, offers users the convenience of shopping, transferring money, and more without the need for a debit or credit card or visiting a physical bank or store. To evoke a sense of confidence, wisdom, and reassurance, the app adopts a clean and minimalist interface predominantly in white, aiming to minimize distractions. The primary color chosen is an elegant shade of very light grey, while blue serves as the secondary color to captivate users' attention. Images are strategically incorporated to enhance the app's seriousness, ensuring a pleasant and user-friendly experience. Selecting the right colors, typography, and styles was a challenging process that involved extensive research on other products and drawing inspiration from fellow UI designers. However, after careful consideration, I successfully determined the ideal choices for my project.
